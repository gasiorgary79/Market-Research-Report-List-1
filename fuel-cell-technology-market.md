<p><p>Fuel cell technology encompasses various types, each with distinct characteristics and applications. Molten Carbonate Fuel Cells (MCFC) operate at high temperatures and are suitable for large-scale power generation. Proton Exchange Membrane Fuel Cells (PEMFC) function at lower temperatures, ideal for vehicles and portable power. Solid Oxide Fuel Cells (SOFC) also work at high temperatures, offering high efficiency for stationary applications. Direct Methanol Fuel Cells (DMFC) use methanol directly, making them convenient for portable applications. Phosphoric Acid Fuel Cells (PAFC) are robust, typically used for distributed power generation.</p></p>
