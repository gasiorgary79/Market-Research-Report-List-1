<p><p>Resistance spot welding machines can be categorized into two main types based on power supply. Single-phase power frequency spot welders typically use a single-phase electrical supply, ideal for smaller applications and operations requiring less power. In contrast, three-phase low-frequency spot welders utilize three-phase power, providing higher efficiency and greater welding capacity, making them suitable for large-scale industrial applications demanding consistent and robust welds. Each type serves specific manufacturing needs, impacting productivity and weld quality.</p></p>
