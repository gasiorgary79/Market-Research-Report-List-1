<p><p>The extra virgin olive oil market is diverse, categorized mainly into four types. Cold-pressed oils, extracted at low temperatures, preserve flavor and nutrients. Flavored oils infuse additional ingredients, enhancing taste for culinary applications. Virgin olive oil, though slightly less pure than extra virgin, maintains good quality through traditional extraction methods. Blended oils combine various olive oils or other fats, often to balance flavor and cost. Together, these categories cater to different consumer preferences, culinary uses, and price points.</p></p>
