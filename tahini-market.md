<p><p>The tahini market primarily consists of two types: hulled and unhulled tahini. Hulled tahini is made from sesame seeds with the outer husk removed, resulting in a smoother texture and milder flavor. It is more popular in culinary applications. Unhulled tahini, made from whole sesame seeds, retains the husk, offering a nuttier taste and higher nutritional value, including more fiber and minerals. Both types cater to diverse consumer preferences, influencing market demand and product offerings.</p></p>
