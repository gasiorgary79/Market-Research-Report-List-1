<p><p>The Waterjet Cutting Head market is primarily divided into two types: abrasive waterjet and pure waterjet cutting. Abrasive waterjet cutting combines high-pressure water with abrasive materials to effectively cut hard materials like metal, stone, and glass, making it suitable for tough applications. In contrast, pure waterjet cutting uses only high-pressure water, ideal for softer materials like rubber, foam, and food products. Both technologies offer precision cutting capabilities, catering to various industries including manufacturing, construction, and automotive.</p></p>
