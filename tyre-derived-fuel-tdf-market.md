<p><p>Tyre Derived Fuel (TDF) is an alternative energy source produced from processed scrap tires, commonly used in industrial applications. The market is primarily divided into two types: shredded tires and whole tires. Shredded TDF involves the preliminary granulation of tires into smaller pieces, enhancing combustion efficiency and facilitating handling. In contrast, whole tire markets involve using intact tires, typically in specialized combustion systems. Both types offer distinct advantages, including reduced landfill waste and lower reliance on conventional fossil fuels.</p></p>
